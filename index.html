Timeline


Chadwick Boseman
"Wakanda Forever"

1976 - Chadwick Boseman was born.
1995 - Chadwick graduated from T.L. Hanna High School.
2000 - Chadwick Boseman graduated from Howard University in Washington, D.C, with a Bachelor's degree in Fine Art and directing.
2003 - Chadwick's first television role was in an episode of Third Watch. He played Reggie Montgomery in the daytime soap opera All My Children.
2008 - Boseman played a prominent role in the television series Lincoln Heights and appeared in his first feature film, The Express: The Ernie Davis Story.
2012 - Chadwick reenacted Jackie Robinson in the movie 42. It tells the story of the legendary baseball player who broke racial barriers by becoming the first African American to play in the major league.
2014 - Other works includes the portrayal of musical legend James Brown in Get On Up: a true story based on James Brown life from his personal journey of struggles to successes.
2016 - Chadwick joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe with his appearance in the superhero ensemble blockbuster Captain America: Civil War.
2018 - Chadwick Boseman played T'challa, a king of a fictional African country named Wakanda, who becomes the most influential leader in Black Panther.
2019 - Black Panther later rejoined Iron man, Thor, Captain America, and other marvel characters for Avengers: Infinity War (2018) and Avengers: Endgame (2019).
2020 - Chadwick Boseman died in Los Angeles, California, at the age of forty-three.
